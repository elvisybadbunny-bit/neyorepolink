## 2. Sales playbook

### Ideal customer profile

Kenyan schools needing connected student, academic, finance, family and campus operations; segment by
education levels, enrollment/staff, day/boarding, current tools, payment readiness and implementation
capacity. Do not claim fit for unsupported jurisdictions/workflows.

### Sales stages

Lead → qualified discovery → demo → instant/formal quote → pilot/trial → decision/contract → live
onboarding → activation → customer success/renewal. Use `SchoolQuoteRequest`, `DemoRequest`, customer
threads and contract records; stage changes need evidence/next action.

### Discovery questions

School structure/curriculum; current painful workflows; student/staff counts; finance/payment;
teacher/family device/connectivity; modules; data migration; decision makers; timeline/budget;
security/compliance; success measure. Never collect credentials in discovery.

### Objection handling

Answer with real behavior, limitations and activation status. Demonstrate tenant isolation,
manual fallback, M-Pesa flow, mobile use and support. Do not promise unbuilt Checklist lines.

## 3. Demo script

1. Confirm prospect context and choose relevant demo tenant.
2. Explain sample data/24-hour isolation.
3. Login as leadership: dashboard, school structure, permissions.
4. Teacher: My Classes, attendance/homework/marks.
5. Finance: fee structure, invoice, STK/reconciliation using safe demo flow.
6. Parent/student: shared portal and row scope.
7. Relevant operations (boarding/library/clinic/etc.).
8. Show reports/print and mobile layout.
9. Explain pricing models/trial, activation dependencies and support.
10. Capture questions, next step, owner/date.

Do not demo founder controls to school users or use production school data.

## 4. Pilot programme guide

Define school, sponsor, dates, users/modules, baseline, success metrics, training, support cadence,
data/privacy, pricing/contract, release whitelist, feedback, exit/convert/export plan. Start with a
bounded workflow; review weekly. A pilot is not a permanent free production entitlement.

## 5. School onboarding guide

Follow Level 07: legal/contact/profile → owner/security → classes/terms/subjects → staff/allocations →
students/guardians → finance/payment → teacher/family proof → relevant modules → publish/go-live →
health check. Validate counts and exceptions before generation/billing.

## 6. Partnership proposal template

Parties/context; shared problem; beneficiaries; scope/deliverables; roles; implementation/pilot;
data/IP/brand; commercial model; measurement/reporting; governance; risks; term/termination;
confidentiality/compliance; next steps. Claims and commitments require authorized approval.

## 7. MoU template outline

Purpose, parties, non-binding/binding clauses, activities, responsibilities, costs, data protection,
confidentiality, IP/brand, governance, term, termination, dispute/law, signatures. Obtain counsel;
do not copy this outline as signed legal language.

## 8. Service agreement template outline

Services/modules, implementation, customer responsibilities, fees/tax/payment, term/renewal,
support/SLA, data processing/security, acceptable use, IP, confidentiality, warranties/limits,
suspension/termination, export/retention, dispute/governing law, schedules and signatures. Use
`NeyoContract` only with approved versioned body.

## 9. Pricing guide

Explain Capacity Complete and Modular User & Module, real count inputs, trial, module proration,
renewal/reprice notice, approved discounts/referrals/SMS top-ups, taxes/payment, grace/suspension and
data preservation. Pull values from current pricing engine/catalog; never hardcode a brochure that
can silently diverge.

## 10. Sales FAQ starter

- What is NEYO? Kenya-first multi-tenant School OS.
- Does it support CBC/CBE and 8-4-4? Yes, current implemented scope in Checklist.
- Is school data mixed? No; tenant/role/row isolation, subject to verified controls.
- How is payment made? Current NEYO subscription central M-Pesa flow; schools have separate fee
  collection.
- Is every integration live automatically? No; provider credentials/approval may be required.
- What happens after trial? Choose pricing model and activate; lifecycle/grace applies.
- Does suspension delete records? No.
- Can NEYO promise a requested feature/date? Only after approved roadmap/contract.

## 11. Brand guidelines and logo use

Canonical: `docs/BRAND.md`, `public/brand/`, `NeyoLogo`, design tokens. Navy/green/warm white,
Inter, restraint, Liquid Glass digital surfaces, plain print. Maintain clear space, aspect ratio and
contrast; never recolor/distort/add effects or place over unreadable backgrounds. School co-branding
must not imply school ownership of NEYO or expose another tenant.

## 12. Tone of voice

Specific, calm, respectful, Kenyan-contextual, action-oriented. Say “Mark 32 students present,” not
“revolutionize attendance.” Avoid fear, blame, generic hype, unsupported superlatives and technical
provider jargon. Product copy says Bundi, never AI. Errors explain what happened and safe next step.

## 13. Social media guide

Content pillars: real workflow education, school operations tips, product releases, customer stories
with consent, Kenyan education context, security/trust, founder learning. Require fact/source,
consent, safe screenshot, brand review, channel format, owner, publish date and response plan. Never
post child data, customer debt, credentials or unreleased incidents/features.

## 14. Content calendar template

Date/channel/campaign/audience/pillar/title/format/CTA/asset/consent/source/owner/status/URL/result.
Monthly review ties metrics to qualified leads/helpfulness, not impressions alone.

## 15. Press kit and launch checklist

Press kit: approved company boilerplate, founder bio/photo, logo pack, product screenshots, facts and
contact. Launch: production/security/legal/provider readiness; support coverage; demo; pricing;
onboarding; status/incident communication; analytics; backups/rollback; announcements; post-launch
monitoring. Do not publish unverified customer/learner counts.

## 16. Case study template

Consent; school context (minimum identifying detail); prior workflow/baseline; implementation;
measured outcome and period; direct approved quote; limitations; screenshots; approval/date. Do not
imply causation from anecdote or reveal learner financial/health/academic data.

## 17. Financial model framework

Model schools by status/size/pricing mode; recurring/term revenue; discounts/referrals/SMS; payment
fees; hosting/database/storage/communications/support/personnel/legal/marketing; gross margin;
collections timing; churn/conversion; cash/runway; scenarios and assumptions. Reconcile to provider,
bank and accounting evidence. `NeyoMetricSnapshot`/`NeyoCostSnapshot` are inputs, not audited books.

## 18. Annual budget and cash-flow forecast

Budget: monthly revenue/cost/capex/headcount/tax/contingency by owner, approved version, actual and
variance. Cash forecast: opening cash, collections timing, operating outflows, statutory/tax,
capex/financing, closing cash, runway, downside triggers. Accountant/board approval as required.

## 19. Pricing calculations and cost tracking

Use current engine functions/config and retain quote snapshots. Cost tracker records provider,
category, invoice period, usage unit, fixed/variable, currency/KES conversion, owner, contract,
allocation and evidence. Monitor R2, DB, Vercel/Fly, SMS/WhatsApp/email, payment, observability and
Bundi provider costs.

## 21. Employee handbook framework

Mission/values; employment categories; working expectations; compensation/benefits; leave; conduct,
anti-harassment and safeguarding; confidentiality/data/security/acceptable use; conflicts; remote
work/equipment; performance/training; grievance/discipline; whistleblowing; separation. Must be

## 22. Recruitment, interviews and job descriptions

Approved role need/budget → job description/outcomes/authority/security access → fair sourcing →
structured scorecard/interview → references/eligibility → written offer/contract → onboarding.
Interview questions remain job-related; minimize applicant data. Job description includes mission,
outcomes, responsibilities, required/preferred skills, reporting, location/terms, access risk and
success measures.

## 23. Employee onboarding, performance, training and exit

Onboarding: contract/policies, identity/payroll, equipment, role access/2FA, security/privacy,
product/customer context, 30/60/90 outcomes. Performance review: objectives/evidence, values,
customer/security quality, strengths/gaps, development/actions. Training logs competence and
completion. Exit: written process, handover, revoke access, return assets, final pay/statutory,
confidentiality/IP reminder and record retention.

## 24. Legal document register

Existing: public Terms and Privacy pages; cookie banner; `NeyoContract` workflow. Required
counsel-approved library may include Cookie Policy, Data Processing Agreement, SLA, NDA, employment
contract, contractor agreement, IP assignment, MoU/service agreement. For each: owner, legal

## 25. AI usage policy (internal)

Approved purposes: drafting/research/code assistance with human verification and safe data.
Prohibited: secrets, unapproved personal/school data, autonomous high-risk decisions, plagiarism/
license violations, bypassing security, presenting generated claims as verified. Record material
provider/model/prompt/version/evaluation where output affects product or corporate decisions.
Product-facing intelligent helper remains Bundi.

## 26. AI prompt library and version history

For each approved prompt: id/domain/purpose/owner; input classification; system/instructions;
expected structured output; forbidden content; examples using synthetic data; model/provider;
version/change/rationale; evaluation set/results; human review; release/retirement. Secrets and real
sensitive examples are excluded.

## 27. Prompt engineering guide

Define task/context/constraints/source authority/output schema/uncertainty/refusal. Ask for citations
or structured fields; separate extraction from decision; minimize context; use deterministic
validation; test adversarial/ambiguous Kenyan examples; version material changes. Never rely on a
prompt to enforce tenant authorization—server code does that.

## 28. AI workflow and verification checklist

Document trigger → data selection/minimization → provider/local processing → output parsing →
confidence → human review → domain validation → commit/audit → retention/metrics/fallback. Verify
facts/code/API, tenant/permissions, calculations, bias/safety, privacy, uncertainty, manual path and
actual tests.

## 29. Human review process and limitations register

High-risk outputs require named authorized reviewer, source comparison, corrections/rejection,
reason and audit. Limitations register: workflow/model/version, known failure modes, affected groups,
severity, mitigation/manual fallback, monitoring, owner/review date. Examples include OCR
handwriting ambiguity and fuzzy record matching; do not invent unobserved defects.

## 30. AI model evaluation log

Date/model/provider/version/config; task/prompt version; synthetic/de-identified evaluation set;
accuracy/error/confidence/review/cost/latency; safety/privacy/adversarial results; comparison;
release decision and reviewer. Re-evaluate on model/prompt/pipeline change.

## 31. Founder personal knowledge continuation

Level 17 defines Decision Log, Lessons, Meetings, Research, School Visits, Feedback, Competitors,
Ideas, Vision Journal and Quarterly Reflections. Keep entries dated, sourced, classified and linked
to actions. Personal thoughts remain drafts until approved through decision/product governance.

This level is an artifact map and template library. Create separate controlled documents when an

financial, HR, marketing or AI-governance material approved merely because a template exists.
