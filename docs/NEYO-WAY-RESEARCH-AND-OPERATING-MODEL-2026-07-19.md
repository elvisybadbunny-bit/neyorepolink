# The NEYO Way — research and operating model

Date: 19 July 2026

NEYO is pre-launch, so retention, engagement, task completion, pilot conversion and exact school value are more decision-useful than vanity registration totals. Current SaaS operating guidance recommends separate daily, weekly and monthly dashboard layers with a small trusted metric set, and pre-product-market-fit focus on retention, engagement and time to value: https://growigami.com/blog/saas-startup-metrics and https://www.dualentry.com/blog/saas-metrics-dashboard.

The integrated playbook translates that into company actions rather than unsupported benchmark claims. It also recognises Kenya-specific professional review areas. A Kenyan startup compliance checklist highlights BRS returns, tax records, licensing and ODPC processes: https://mukambalaw.com/startup-compliance-checklist/. Tax treatment must be confirmed with a Kenyan accountant; third-party summaries about VAT or digital services are not treated as legal conclusions.

## Governing rule

No feature is standard merely because it exists in code. It becomes standard after a real authorised school user completes it safely, value and cost are measured, permissions and tenant isolation are verified, mobile and fallback workflows work, documentation and support exist, and rollback is possible.

## Founder constraint rule

NEYO must be operable alongside university, health and sleep. The founder should retain product and customer truth while delegating licensed/specialist judgments. Before live schools, identify an authorised emergency technical backup and advisors in school operations, Kenyan law/privacy, accounting/tax and security.
