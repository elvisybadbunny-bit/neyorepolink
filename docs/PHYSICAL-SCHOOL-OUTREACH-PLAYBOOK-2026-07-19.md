# NEYO Physical School Outreach Playbook

Physical visits are sensible for an early Kenyan school product because they produce direct workflow learning and trust before paid advertising. They are not automatically cheap: track travel, preparation, meeting time, follow-up time and conversion.

## Recommended visit sequence

1. Build a list of schools matching the current ideal profile.
2. Call or write for an appointment; do not rely on an unannounced visit.
3. Confirm the decision-maker and a 20–30 minute discovery slot.
4. Prepare one school-relevant demonstration using demonstration data.
5. Ask about the current workflow before presenting features.
6. Agree one next action with a date and owner.
7. Send a short recap only when follow-up permission was given.
8. Update the CRM on the same day.

## Questions worth asking

- Which school operation consumes the most staff time each week?
- How are unmatched Paybill payments handled?
- How long do attendance and term results take?
- Which records are duplicated between paper and spreadsheets?
- What failed with the current or previous system?
- Who uses, approves, pays for and can block a new system?
- What evidence would justify a one-month pilot?
- When is the least disruptive implementation window?

## Do not do

- Do not collect real learner data during the sales meeting.
- Do not promise every feature, compliance, integrations or uptime without evidence.
- Do not offer an indefinite free pilot.
- Do not record gossip or unrelated personal information.
- Do not mark polite interest as a sale.
- Do not spend heavily travelling without measuring qualified meetings and conversions.
