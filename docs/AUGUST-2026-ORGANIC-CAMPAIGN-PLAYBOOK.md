# NEYO August 2026 Organic Campaign Playbook

**Campaign:** 1–30 August 2026  
**Publishing days:** Monday–Friday, 3–28 August (20 days)  
**Rest days:** Saturdays and Sundays  
**Output:** 8 original short videos per weekday = **160 videos**; each may be cross-posted to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels and Facebook Reels.  
**Commercial objective:** build a permission-based Term 3 pilot waitlist; learn from real schools; gather evidence for a September pilot; use pilot evidence—not hype—to prepare for a wider January 2027 academic-year launch.

## 1. Non-negotiable truth boundary

Every video must distinguish:

- built and demonstrated locally;
- statically verified but awaiting a migrated preview;
- browser-tested;
- externally dependent;
- not yet proven.

Never claim:

- Ministry/KNEC/KRA/NTSA/ODPC approval or integration without evidence;
- production activation;
- live M-Pesa success when using a simulator;
- camera/printer/hardware proof without the device;
- a school partnership without written permission;
- testimonials, user counts, savings or outcomes not measured;
- that NEYO uses or submits to government portals when it does not.

Use fictional data only. Blur browser URLs, phone numbers, credentials, tokens and real learner details.

## 2. The realistic production system

Eight completely separate polished productions daily is unsustainable for a two-person, zero-revenue team. Produce one 60–90 minute screen-recording block each morning and cut it into eight focused 25–50 second videos.

### Daily cadence

| Time | Work |
|---|---|
| 07:00–07:30 | Review scripts, reset fictional demo state |
| 07:30–09:00 | Record one coherent workflow in 8 segments |
| 09:00–11:00 | Edit captions, crop, privacy check, export |
| 11:00–11:30 | Schedule first four posts |
| 12:30–14:00 | School visit or outreach block |
| 14:00–15:00 | Schedule remaining four posts |
| 16:30–17:00 | Reply to comments; record FAQs and leads |
| 17:00–17:20 | Metrics and next-day corrections |

Suggested posting windows to test, not guaranteed “best times”:

```text
07:15, 08:30, 10:00, 11:45, 13:15, 15:30, 18:00, 20:15
```

Do not publish identical files simultaneously everywhere. Change the first sentence/caption and allow 10–20 minutes between platforms.

## 3. Video structure

### 25–50 second short

1. **0–2 sec — Hook:** a school problem, contradiction or visible result.
2. **2–6 sec — Context:** name the role and workflow.
3. **6–32 sec — Proof:** one uninterrupted click path.
4. **32–42 sec — Boundary:** what is controlled, pending or not automatic.
5. **Final 3–6 sec — One CTA:** waitlist, pilot brief, school visit or official link.

### Visual rules

- 9:16, 1080×1920 export;
- captions always on;
- 42px-equivalent minimum caption size;
- one highlighted cursor/tap target;
- no more than two lines of caption at once;
- use a real small-phone capture for parent/teacher mobile claims;
- use plain A4 capture for print claims;
- no copyrighted trending audio unless each platform licenses its use;
- use NEYO-owned voice, ambient silence or platform-cleared sound;
- school-facing copy says Bundi, never provider names;
- end card under 2 seconds: `Term 3 pilot waitlist · neyo.co.ke`.

## 4. Platform packaging

### TikTok / Instagram Reels

- Lead with the problem, not “Hi, we are NEYO.”
- Caption: 1–2 sentences and 3–5 relevant hashtags.
- Pin one comment: pilot location, school type sought and waitlist link.

### YouTube Shorts

- Searchable title: `How a Kenyan school can… | NEYO School OS`.
- Description: workflow, evidence boundary, waitlist link.
- Put related Shorts in weekly playlists.

### Facebook Reels

- Add two sentences for school owners/teachers who watch without sound.
- Share into groups only where promotion is allowed; never spam groups.
- Ask administrators before repeatedly posting.

## 5. Waitlist funnel

A view is not a lead. Every CTA should land on a simple form collecting only:

- school name;
- county/sub-county;
- day/boarding/both;
- levels;
- approximate learners/staff;
- contact person, role, +254 phone and email;
- top three problems;
- preferred demonstration date;
- consent to be contacted;
- source video/platform.

Stages:

```text
NEW → CONTACT_PERMISSION_CONFIRMED → DISCOVERY_BOOKED → SCHOOL_VISITED
→ PILOT_REVIEW → PILOT_APPROVED / NOT_NOW / NOT_FIT
```

Do not add scraped school phone numbers to marketing lists. Public contact details may be used for one respectful institutional introduction; continued messaging needs permission.

## 6. Team split

### Founder — product and trust

- records product proof;
- handles technical/academic questions;
- conducts principal/Director of Studies discovery;
- approves claims before publishing;
- owns pilot scope and final decisions.

### Sales and marketing colleague

Before calling the person NEYO’s “first employee,” use a written arrangement reviewed for Kenyan employment/tax obligations. Define whether this is employment, internship, commission-based agency or volunteer help; do not leave compensation vague.

Suggested responsibilities:

- prepare shot list and demo reset;
- edit/caption/schedule;
- maintain consent-based lead tracker;
- call to confirm school-visit appointments;
- record objections and follow-up dates;
- never negotiate pricing/commit features outside written authority;
- never receive school money personally.

### Daily 15-minute review

- What was published?
- Any privacy/accuracy issue?
- Which hook retained viewers?
- Which comments indicate buyer intent?
- Which leads consented to contact?
- What should stop or change tomorrow?

## 7. School visits — five per week

Target **20 appointments**, not 20 unannounced gate visits. One school each weekday is the upper practical limit alongside eight videos.

Weekly pattern:

| Day | Visit focus |
|---|---|
| Monday | Principal/owner: pain discovery + 3-workflow demo |
| Tuesday | Director of Studies: timetable, marks, reports |
| Wednesday | Bursar/admin: fees, payments, imports |
| Thursday | Teachers/ICT: daily work, mobile, offline |
| Friday | Follow-up school or boarding operations |

### Appointment request

> Hello, my name is Elvis Malimbe, founder of NEYO School OS. We are inviting a small number of Kenyan schools to review a Term 3 pilot focused on connected school workflows such as timetable, attendance, reports and finance. We are not asking you to purchase during the visit. May we book 30 minutes during the August holiday to understand your current process and demonstrate three relevant workflows?

### 30-minute visit

1. 0–5: ask about school, no laptop.
2. 5–10: identify top three pains.
3. 10–22: show only three matching workflows.
4. 22–26: explain pilot boundaries and pending evidence.
5. 26–30: agree next action, owner and date.

Carry:

- charged laptop and phone;
- offline screenshots/video fallback;
- one-page pilot brief;
- privacy/data-handling summary;
- workflow checklist;
- contact/consent form;
- no production credentials.

## 8. Weekly objectives

### Week 1 — recognition and core school setup

Prove NEYO is a connected Kenyan school operating system, not a template dashboard.

### Week 2 — academic depth

Timetable, Senior School, assessments, exams and reports.

### Week 3 — whole-school value

Library, communication, operations, parent experience, offline/privacy.

### Week 4 — trust and pilot conversion

Public website, leadership, pilot terms, campaign evidence and next steps.

## 9. Metrics that matter

Record by video and platform:

- views;
- 3-second hold rate where provided;
- average watch time / completion;
- saves and shares;
- profile visits;
- waitlist clicks;
- completed consented leads;
- booked discovery meetings;
- attended school visits;
- pilot-review candidates.

Do not optimise primarily for raw views. Primary campaign measure:

```text
qualified pilot-review schools / completed waitlist schools
```

Secondary measures:

```text
visit booking rate
visit attendance rate
video-to-waitlist conversion
waitlist-to-discovery conversion
```

## 10. Stop rules

Pause publishing if:

- a real learner/staff record appears;
- a credential/token appears;
- a workflow repeatedly errors;
- a legal/approval claim is challenged and unverified;
- comments identify a safeguarding concern;
- the waitlist form loses consent/source data;
- production volume harms product stability or founder health.

Rest weekends. Batch Monday content on Friday if needed. A campaign that exhausts the two-person team before pilot onboarding is a failed campaign.
